Fatima, a British-Lebanese mother living in the UK, wanted her 7-year-old daughter, Laila, to connect with her Arab heritage. "I speak Arabic to her at home," Fatima says, "but I wanted her to have a more structured, and most importantly, fun way to learn. I didn't want it to feel like school."

She was looking for a program that could capture a child's imagination. After trying a few options, she discovered the Arabic for Kids program at Arabfield Academy.

Learning Through Play

Laila's trial lesson was filled with songs, colorful pictures, and a puppet named 'Kiko' who only spoke Arabic. "Laila was completely captivated," Fatima recalls. "She wasn't being 'taught'; she was playing a game with her new friend and her tutor."

The curriculum is built around interactive activities. Laila learns vocabulary through virtual "shopping trips," practices her letters by "finding treasure" on a map, and learns to form sentences by telling stories about her favorite animals.

"Laila doesn't even realize she's 'learning'. She just thinks she's having her weekly fun session with her tutor. It's been wonderful to watch her confidence grow. She now proudly shows off new words to her grandparents on video calls."

Sparking a Lifelong Love

The goal of the kids' program is not just to teach the language, but to spark a lifelong love for it. By associating Arabic with joy, creativity, and play, it builds a positive foundation that will stay with the child for years to come.

"The best part is hearing her mix Arabic words into her English sentences naturally," says Fatima. "It shows that the language is becoming a real part of her world. We're not just giving her a skill; we're giving her a piece of her identity."
